About Me

Hi, I’m Maul. I started my journey in tech back in 2015 when I studied Information Technology (S1) at Universitas Riau (UNRI). At that time, I was still exploring different areas and trying to understand what really interested me in software development.

Around late 2019, I began experimenting with game development using Unity. It started as curiosity, but it quickly became something I genuinely enjoyed. I spent time learning how game systems work, building small projects, and exploring ideas—one of them being a turn-based game where I implemented Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) to simulate and predict the best moves against the player.

In 2024, I started working as a developer at a state-owned enterprise (BUMN). My main focus is backend development, working with technologies like Go, Node.js, and Spring Boot to build and maintain systems. I’ve also had some exposure to frontend development and contributed to projects using Flutter in my workplace.

One of the projects I’ve been involved in is an internal AI chatbot system designed to provide company-related information. In this project, I worked mainly on the backend side—handling APIs, system integration, and ensuring the service runs reliably. The system is currently used internally, with plans to expand it for public use in the future.

As I continued working, I became more interested in how systems are managed and deployed. This led me to explore DevOps practices—working with tools like GitHub, Docker, and Jenkins to understand automation, containerization, and deployment workflows.

Recently, I’ve been exploring simpler and more efficient ways to build web applications, which led me to experiment with HTMX for personal projects. For me, learning has always been about trying things out, building real projects, and improving step by step—and this website is part of that journey.
